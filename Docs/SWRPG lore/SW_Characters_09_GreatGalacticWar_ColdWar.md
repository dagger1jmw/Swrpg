\---

## ABOUT THIS DOCUMENT

Character reference for the **SWTOR era** — the **Great Galactic War** (3,681–3,653 BBY), the **Cold War** (3,653–3,642 BBY), and the renewed **Galactic War** (3,642–3,636 BBY) between the resurgent Sith Empire and the Galactic Republic. **This is the era your game is set in (3,661 BBY — year 20 of the Great Galactic War).** Drawn from *Star Wars: The Old Republic* (BioWare/EA), *The Old Republic: Revan*, *Deceived*, *Annihilation*, *Fatal Alliance*, the *Threat of Peace* / *Blood of the Empire* comics, and supporting Legends sources.

**This doc delivers Vitiate's true god-emperor apex sheet**, plus the freed Revan and Lord Scourge sheets flagged in Era 08.

Stats represent each character **at their prime**, on the **galaxy-wide Legends scale**.

### Stats in use (v3 — six core, five Force)

**Core:** Strength · Agility · Endurance · Willpower · Charisma · **Intelligence**. **Force:** ForceSense · Meditation · ForceKnowledge · ForceControl · **ForceOutput**.

* **Intelligence** — **Vitiate** (1,400-year manipulator, 120) and the schemers **Darth Baras / Darth Jadus / Cipher Nine** top the era. **Revan** remains a constant 100.
* **ForceOutput** = raw power/magnitude. Vitiate's drain magnitude is enormous, but see the apex-ceiling note: his *galactic drain* is a specialist ability, not a license to exceed peak Luke overall.

### ⚑ APEX CEILING — the top of the entire Legends power hierarchy (reference)

This is the project's master power-scaling anchor. It governs Vitiate here and every apex being in later eras.

|Tier|Band|Who|Note|
|-|-|-|-|
|**TRANSCENDENT — off the scale**|**151+**|**Abeloth ≈ 185** *(nominal floor — effectively beyond measurement)*|**In a tier of her own, far above every other being in Legends.** Stated to be \~**a dozen times** peak Luke in the Force; nearly crushed apex Luke's throat through his full resistance; her *diluted, galaxy-spread avatars* each pushed apex Luke to his limit; fed on the Font of Power + Pool of Knowledge for \~100,000 years. **No single being could defeat her** — it took **peak Luke + Darth Krayt together** (Era 17) merely to *exile* her. The Mortis "Ones" (Era 12, \~140–144) sit far below her, in raw cosmic terms, but see below.|
|**S — Realized mortal apex**|**135–142**|**Peak Luke Skywalker ≈ 138** (post-Vong / Fate of the Jedi, Era 17)|The strongest *realized mortal* Force user in history, and the realized ceiling of the Chosen One bloodline's shared potential. Nothing realized-mortal exceeds him — **only the off-the-scale Abeloth is above him**, and by an enormous margin.|
|**A — Apex legendary**|**126–135**|**Vitiate ≈ 134 ceiling**, Darth Krayt ≈ 132 (Era 18), **Emperor Palpatine ≈ 130** (Era 13; Dark Empire clone higher still)|Each terrifying; each **below peak Luke overall.** Just below this band sit **Knightfall Anakin ≈ 120 Output** (the observed pre-suit peak — still growing, not full power) and **Darth Bane (Era 10) ≈ 114**, at the top of standard legendary.|

**Rulings applied across the series:**

* **Luke is the strongest realized Force user ever, second only to Abeloth.** In Legends he was stated to be the most powerful; George Lucas confirmed Luke shares Anakin's Force potential, so Grandmaster Luke represents the full realization of what the Chosen One could reach.
* **Anakin never freely reached that ceiling** — cut down at Mustafar, then the suit capped Vader well below. The most we ever *observed* of him is **Knightfall (\~120 Output)** — powerful but still growing, short of full strength. His *theoretical* full potential (\~138) equals what Luke realized, and was only ever glimpsed on **Mortis**, where the Force-nexus realm briefly unlocked it and let him best the Son. Vader (suited) is lower still in raw Output.
* **Mortis "Ones" (Era 12, \~140–144):** powerful cosmic embodiments, but since a near-full-potential Anakin overcame the Son on Mortis, a realized-peak Luke is treated as **≥ the Ones**, leaving **the off-the-scale Abeloth the sole clear superior to Luke.**
* **Vitiate is the apex dark-side *specialist*, not the apex being.** His galaxy-scale life-drain is rated as a specialist **ability \~145** (the single most destructive ability in the timeline), but his **overall stat profile stays below peak Luke** (ceiling \~134). Same philosophy as Nihilus (Era 08): legendary in one dimension, not above everyone overall.

## Vitiate — the Sith Emperor (APEX — recalibrated below peak Luke)

* **Sheet snapshot:** **True apex prime** — the immortal god-emperor at the height of his power (Tier A apex legendary, ceiling \~134)
* **Species / Affiliation / Rank:** Sith (human-presenting); an ancient dark-side entity · reconstituted Sith Empire · Dark Lord of the Sith / Sith Emperor (and secretly far more)
* **Lifespan:** Born Tenebrae \~5,113 BBY (Era 03); ruled the reconstituted Empire \~1,400+ years; host body destroyed by the Hero of Tython (3,637 BBY); persisted toward his Valkorion/Eternal-Empire incarnation (Era 12)
* **Summary:** The single most powerful dark-side master in galactic history — though, per the apex ceiling above, **the apex dark-side *specialist*, not the apex being** (peak Luke surpasses him overall; only Abeloth surpasses Luke). After founding the reconstituted Empire on Dromund Kaas (Era 03), Tenebrae shed his name to rule for over fourteen centuries as the unseen **Sith Emperor** — a being who drained the entire world of Nathema (and 8,000 Sith Lords) to make himself immortal, and who orchestrated galactic history across millennia: the Mandalorian Wars (a test), the corruption of Revan and Malak, and the Great Galactic War. His true goal was never conquest but **apotheosis** — to perform his life-draining ritual on a *galactic* scale and become a true immortal god. He drained whole planets (Nathema; later Ziost, as a spirit), dominated minds en masse, reshaped Dromund Kaas's sky into a permanent storm, and survived death by body-hopping and spirit-persistence.

A combined Republic–Imperial force (Satele and Marr) defeated him there. His core power remained at his legendary apex; his fractured mind made him erratic and tragic.
